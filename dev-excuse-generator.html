The Five Pillars of Developer Excuses

Every developer excuse in history belongs to one of five canonical categories, each with its own logic and use case:

  • 💾 Classic Deflection — The timeless staples. "It works on my machine." "That's not a bug, that's a feature." These are the load-bearing pillars of developer culture.
  • 🕵️ Git Archaeology — The blame lies in the commit history. Specifically in a commit written before you joined and touched by someone who no longer works here.
  • 🔥 Infrastructure Gremlins — It's DNS. It's the cache. It's the load balancer doing something nobody understands. The servers have feelings and today they're sad.
  • 📋 Scope Creep — The requirements changed. Again. After you finished. The original ticket no longer describes what was actually asked for.
  • 🌌 Cosmic Forces — Mercury is in retrograde. Solar flares flipped a bit. The universe is simply not aligned for this feature to work today.

Frequently Asked Questions

The Classic and Infrastructure categories are largely indistinguishable from real developer communication. The Cosmic Forces category requires a very understanding team. Use the "honest translation" toggle before deploying any excuse to a professional context to assess risk.
