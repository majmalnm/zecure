Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 12, 2026  ·  Effective: April 12, 2026  ·  Questions? info@zecure.net

Plain-English summary (TL;DR)

Zecure generates passwords and keys entirely inside your browser using the Web Crypto API. We have no backend, no database, and no analytics. We do not collect, store, or transmit any of the values you generate — or any personal information about you. The only external request this page makes is loading fonts from Google Fonts.

1. Who We Are

Zecure ("we", "us", or "our") operates the website at zecure.net — a free, browser-based password and cryptographic key generator.

For any privacy-related enquiries, contact us at info@zecure.net.

2. Information We Do Not Collect

Because all generation is performed client-side (in your browser), we have no technical means of collecting the following, and we do not attempt to:

  • Passwords or keys you generate
  • Any text you copy to your clipboard
  • Your IP address (no server-side logging)
  • Browser fingerprint or device identifiers
  • Your name, email address, or any account information (there is no account system)
  • Cookies for tracking purposes

3. Information That May Be Collected

The following limited, non-personal data may be processed as a side-effect of normal web infrastructure:

3.1 Google Fonts
This site loads Inter and JetBrains Mono from Google Fonts (fonts.googleapis.com). When your browser requests a font file, Google's servers receive your IP address and User-Agent header as part of the standard HTTP request. This is governed by Google's Privacy Policy. To avoid this entirely, you may use the site offline after the fonts have been cached, or self-host the fonts.

3.2 Hosting infrastructure
If you access Zecure through GitHub Pages or any CDN, that provider's standard access logs (IP address, timestamp, requested URL, response code) may be retained per their own privacy policy. Zecure does not have access to these logs.

3.3 localStorage
Zecure stores a single key in your browser's localStorage: your theme preference (zecure-theme, value: "dark" or "light"). This data never leaves your device. You can clear it at any time by clearing your browser's site data.

4. How Generated Values Are Handled

All passwords, keys, and tokens are generated using window.crypto.getRandomValues() — the browser's built-in cryptographically secure pseudo-random number generator (CSPRNG). The entire process runs inside your browser's JavaScript sandbox:

  • No network request is made during or after generation
  • Generated values are never written to disk, sent to a server, or logged
  • Values exist only in memory and are discarded when the page is refreshed or closed
  • The copy-to-clipboard action writes to the local OS clipboard only — nothing else

5. Cookies

Zecure does not set any cookies. No session cookies, no tracking cookies, no third-party cookies. The theme preference described in Section 3.3 is stored in localStorage, not a cookie.

6. Third-Party Links

The blog articles on this site contain links to external resources (NIST, Google, GitHub, etc.). Once you follow a link to another website, this Privacy Policy no longer applies. We encourage you to review the privacy policy of any site you visit.

7. Children's Privacy

Zecure does not collect personal information from anyone, including children under 13 (or the applicable age of digital consent in your jurisdiction). Because no data is collected at all, there is no special risk to younger users. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us at info@zecure.net and we will take appropriate action.

8. Your Rights

Because we do not collect personal data, many data-subject rights (access, rectification, erasure) are satisfied by design — there is nothing for us to retrieve, correct, or delete. If you are a resident of the EU/EEA (GDPR), UK (UK GDPR), or California (CCPA), and believe we hold any data about you, please contact info@zecure.net and we will respond within 30 days.

9. Data Security

Given that no personal data is transmitted to or stored on any server operated by Zecure, there is no central data store to breach. The security of generated values is entirely within your control once they leave the browser. We recommend:

  • Storing generated passwords in a reputable password manager
  • Never sharing generated keys via unencrypted channels (plain email, SMS)
  • Treating any generated secret as sensitive from the moment it is created
